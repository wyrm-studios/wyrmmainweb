How It All Started — The WYRM x Djappa Man Story

I still remember the day Djappa Man reached out to us. For those who don't know, this man is a legend. He's the founder of ZOMRA – the crew that literally built the foundation of underground hip-hop in Tunisia. Anti-system, raw, real – that's ZOMRA. That's Djappa Man.

But here's the thing: he had been gone for a long time. Years away from the scene. No rap, no art, completely silent. When someone like that disappears, you feel it in the culture.

Then out of nowhere, he hit us up at WYRM. "I'm back," he told us. "I want to create something new. I need a complete branding – something personal, something that represents who I am now."

I won't lie – I was honored. Nervous too. This is Djappa Man we're talking about. The guy who pioneered the movement. And he chose us to bring his vision back to life. He trusted us with his legacy.

This wasn't just another project. This was history in the making. And it all started with one message: "I'm back in the game."

The Challenge

After years away from the scene, Djappa Man needed a complete visual rebrand that honored his legendary status in Tunisian hip-hop while representing his evolution as an artist. The brand needed to work across multiple platforms — podcast series, music releases, social media, and live streams.

The Solution

Complete brand identity system including character design, Mental Files podcast branding with animated intro, music cover designs, thumbnails, and a cohesive visual language that bridges his underground roots with his current artistic vision.

Outcome

Complete visual identity for Mental Files podcast series, music releases, and social media presence. Ongoing collaboration from 2023-2025 building a cohesive brand that represents Djappa Man's legacy and evolution.

Strategy

The strategy focused on honoring Djappa Man's legendary status in the Tunisian underground hip-hop scene while creating a fresh, modern visual identity that represents his return and evolution. The brand needed to be versatile enough to work across podcast content, music releases, and social media while maintaining authenticity and raw energy.
